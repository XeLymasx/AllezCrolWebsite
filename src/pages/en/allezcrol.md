Edward was born in <ruby>N / A.F<rt>Nashville / A.Federation</rt></ruby>(1989), In 2008, he was admitted to the A. Federal Military Academy | Agent Professional College. After graduating in 2012, he was specially invited by the government to join the Federal Parliament as a member of parliament.
Joined in 2014 and participated in negotiation meetings in Country B[^1] as a member of the Senate Foreign Affairs Ambassador Delegation.
From 2016 to 2020, attended major national meetings in the capacity of a member of parliament. In 2022, officially changed the status from a federal parliament member to a representative capacity, and joined V.C.I[^2] Company for employment. Thereafter, held two positions simultaneously, enjoying a prominent status.

## Book

In the Autonomous series, Edward often sat expressionlessly in one of the seats at the back of the auditorium, head bent over a book. He never grew tired of its content, regardless of how many times he had read it from start to finish. Even though the series had released more than thirty volumes, he still preferred the third volume most, followed by the fifth (which he only read for three days before stopping). After work, he frequently went alone to the auditorium, found a seat, and continued reading. He also carried a pen and a ruler, marking the book densely with colorful annotations. With each page he turned, numerous notes would appear, yet even when they affected his reading experience, he never complained, as he had studied the book so thoroughly that he could effortlessly recall every detail contained within it.

As for why Edward is so obsessed with this book, it is simply because so many of the narratives in it mirror the experiences he has encountered in his professional career, as if the protagonist in the book were himself. He believes this cannot be a mere coincidence, and therefore he spends all of his spare time on this book[^3].

## Friends

Edward does not frequently keep in touch with his family (seemingly in a state of separation and non-contact), but occasionally sends his brother Pink[^4] an allowance ranging from 1000 AC[^5] to 1500 AC.
Apart from his relatives, during Edward's tenure as a full-time legislator, he became acquainted with five like-minded allies[^6],
However, after taking up a position at V.C.I, only two allies continued to maintain contact with Edward. Unlike Pink, they often gathered at the POO Café on South Street to have coffee and discuss everyday matters.

After Edward joined V.C.I., because of his status as a member of parliament, many people sought to curry favor with this executive to facilitate their past political careers.
Edward had long seen through the petty intentions of those who approached him to curry favor, so in his daily work, he endeavored to be self-reliant and handle matters personally, without relying on any external assistance.
However, this does not mean that Edward only encounters friends concerned with their own interests within the company. He gets along harmoniously with his colleagues, and even though they are aware of Edward's identity, they remain unaffected. This sincerity has also dispelled Edward's usual suspicions, allowing him to confidently engage in mutual support and cooperation with his colleagues, advancing together hand in hand.

## Smoker

Edward's greatest hobby was, after finishing his work for the day and reading a book, taking out the cigarette he carried with him, striking a match to light it, and lying on a bench by the flowerbed or a seat in the auditorium, slowly enjoying the pleasure the cigarette brought him.

Edward frequently attends events and conferences, venues where fireworks are prohibited, but there are usually designated smoking areas. As a result, on many occasions, Edward chooses not to appear at meetings or events he considers unimportant, but instead spends his time in the smoking areas, where he is often the only person present.

A significant part of Edward's development of his smoking habit stems from his father, who, when Edward was young, would often sit alone right in front of their home smoking after being reprimanded by his superiors, using it to numb his pain at the time and quickly forget those painful memories.

At that time, Edward was still in junior high school. Every day after school, he would see his father sitting directly in front of the house smoking. Sometimes, his father would fall asleep at the doorway, still holding a cigarette that was either unfinished or burnt out. When Edward asked his father why he always smoked when faced with difficulties, his father merely replied perfunctorily, 'I am just too tired.'

## Parliament

Edward's father was also a member of parliament, so from a young age, his father cultivated him into a young 'old hand' in the political arena. After his father's retirement, he successfully inherited his father's position as a member of parliament and worked with Richard P.[^7].

The position of a member of parliament actually contradicted the career that Edward had dreamed of; he wanted to become a soldier and achieve glory for his country on the battlefield. However, Edward's father believed that his physical condition was extremely poor. Apart from possessing an exceptionally sharp mind, his father only wished for him to have a stable and secure job, like himself, leading a life of quiet anonymity.

After Edward completely severed ties with his family, he began to consider developing in other directions, no longer limiting himself to the role of a member of parliament. Edward started to study diligently at the military academy and achieved good results, successfully entering work at V.C.I.

# Personality

Edward would not lose his temper with anyone without reason, not even with the politicians who have mocked him, because wasting words on people he dislikes would only make him feel extremely foolish.
Edward remained calm and composed in the face of many difficult troubles, because he had personally witnessed how his father had thrown away his own future. Many people (including those who did not like him) could not help but praise him: 'When you are with him, the only thing you cannot do is make him look at you with wide, glaring eyes.'

Edward greatly enjoys sharing his belongings with others, such as an exciting book, a delicious dish, or an interesting experience, and he is equally willing to help his colleagues plan shortcut paths in their careers.
However, Edward was also well aware that his actions could create a sense of dependence among his colleagues. Therefore, on many occasions, when Edward was confronted with questions from those closest to him, he would make excuses to evade them, for he understood that he could not allow his friends to become selfish individuals who relied on others' thoughts to pave their own way.

Edward handles every aspect of his work with great care; he is keenly perceptive of any errors in his work and the implications behind others' words. It is precisely this conscientious and responsible attitude that allows him to thrive in the workplace, and other members of the parliament also look up to him as a role model.

Edward is always racing against time for his work. He is an exceptionally intelligent congressman, so the responsibility for handling many important documents in the federal parliament falls entirely on his shoulders. Out of necessity, he has to deal with these tasks during his sleeping hours, and thus his habit of staying up late gradually developed. (However, this does not exclude the possibility that he might browse the internet idly during work hours, as he can be quite playful.)

Edward's work area is always messy, with many documents piled haphazardly on the floor. Due to the nature of his work, he has ordered a large number of mechanical devices, and his studio is filled with numerous file boxes and equipment.
His colleagues all teased him that his office was so messy that there was not even a place to set foot.
Edward does not have the habit of taking out the trash and usually relies on his assistant to do so. However, his assistant's working hours are very irregular, which has resulted in one side of his office being piled with garbage bags, producing a very unpleasant odor. Many colleagues simply place documents on the table outside his office and never proactively enter.

Edward likes to take advantage of certain loopholes in federal parliamentary regulations to secure benefits for his friends. Even though he has repeatedly received stern warnings from the board of the parliament, he has always only complied superficially, while secretly securing every possible benefit for his colleagues.
